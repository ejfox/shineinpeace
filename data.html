To understand how gun violence works in Oakland, we must understand the broader context. This map only paints one part of that picture, and it's a work in progress.

The vast majority of our data is gathered from the Oakland Police Department and only represents shootings reported to the police -- in some cases, victims of gun violence do not wish to talk with police, and as such there are gaps in our demographic data.

There are also occasions where this data is collected from first-hand accounts and news reports.

You can toggle different layers for comparison, such as city council districts, Oakland Police Department beats, and areas with gang injunctions. You can also toggle between homicides, shootings with confirmed injuries, and reported shootings.

We've chosen to include officer-involved shootings in our data set, so killings deemed justified do show up here under the "homicide" map layer. The "shootings with injuries" layer only includes incidents where we have confirmed that victims were hurt, but doesnâ€™t necessarily mean other shootings had no victims.

The map updates as quickly as new details are provided, and victim data is updated as quickly as we can get it. This data doesn't represent all shootings in Oakland -- it's only the floor, a bare minimum. There are likely many more incidents that go unreported. But even if this data were complete, it still wouldn't tell the whole story -- for that, we must rely on the unique, individual stories of those affected by gun violence.
